COVID-19 Restrictions

Check here for the latest restrictions for our big day.

To be sure there is enough room for everyone, the venue has set their max capacity at 100 people. This number does not include their staff, but it does include our other vendors (photographers, videographers, DJs, etc.). We will be in the biggest ballroom, and we are the only wedding reception being hosted that day, so spreading out should be easy as pie!

Regarding masks, the venue is following the indoor dining restrictions. That is, masks must be worn whenever you're inside a building, but not sitting at your table. This includes while in the chapel (seated or standing), trips to the bar, trips to the bathroom, and while mingling with other guests.

Guests will be seated at tables by immediate family (or immediate social group). Only large tables will be used, and guest count per table will be kept small (e.g. 6 person family at a 12 person table). The ballroom is quite large and will allow for even large tables to be spaced comfortably.

While mingling will be allowed, comfort level will vary among our guests, and our top priority is to keep everyone safe and comfortable, so different color wristbands will be provided for guests to clearly display their willingness to socialize without the need for others to approach and ask. All guests are asked to please respect the wristband color worn by others while mingling.

Guest are encouraged to enjoy the night and "let the music move you!" While the center of the dance floor will be off limits, dancing near the guests’ own tables will be declared groovy zones!

Upon entering the building, guests will have their temperatures taken via touchless thermometer. Any guest exhibiting symptoms will be asked to show their love from afar.

Guests are asked to abide by any and all social distancing guidelines which they would follow at any public venue, like supermarkets, restaurants, department stores, etc. We'll do our best to keep everyone safe and comfortable, but we're relying on each of our guests to aid us in this effort.

We understand that a few graphics and some text cannot possibly address everyone's concerns, so please, if you have any questions or concerns, contact us directly.
